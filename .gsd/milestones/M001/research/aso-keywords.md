# ASO Keyword Research — WrenchLog

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Category:** Utilities / Lifestyle (Auto)
**Platform:** iOS (App Store)

---

## Summary

The car maintenance app market on iOS is moderately competitive with a handful of established players (CARFAX Car Care, Simply Auto, Drivvo, Fuelly, ServiceLog, FIXD) but no dominant brand-name monopoly on keywords. Most competitors use generic, keyword-dense titles and subtitles. Privacy-focused, indie-friendly positioning is an emerging differentiator. Search drives ~65–70% of all App Store downloads, making keyword optimization critical for organic growth.

### Patterns Observed

1. **Title formula:** Most top apps use `[Brand]: [Primary Keyword]` or `[Primary Keyword]: [Brand]` format
2. **"Car Maintenance"** appears in title of 4/10 top competitors — it's the highest-value phrase
3. **Subtitles** stack 2–3 secondary keywords (fuel log, mileage tracker, service reminders)
4. **About 41% of top iOS apps** include generic keywords beyond brand name in titles (AppTweak, 2025)
5. **Privacy** is emerging as a differentiator — ServiceLog and reviews explicitly call out "your data stays yours"

## Keyword Gap Analysis

### Keywords Competitors Miss

1. **"car maintenance log"** — No top app uses this exact phrase despite it being the most natural way a user would describe what they want. ServiceLog comes closest but uses "service log" instead.

2. **"vehicle service history"** — CARFAX partially covers this via their VIN history feature, but pure maintenance trackers don't target it. Strong resale-value angle.

3. **"auto maintenance reminder"** — The "auto" variant is underused. Most apps use "car" exclusively, missing users who search with "auto" or "automobile."

4. **"DIY car maintenance"** — None of the top apps position for the DIY/home mechanic audience explicitly. WrenchLog's name naturally appeals to this segment.

5. **"car maintenance cost tracker"** — The expense angle combined with maintenance is undertargeted. Most apps split these into separate features without combining in keywords.

6. **"private car maintenance app"** — Privacy is a growing concern (CARFAX collects data for their business model). No app explicitly targets the "private" keyword.

7. **"car maintenance no subscription"** — Users frustrated with subscription fatigue (Carly gets bad reviews for this). Lifetime pricing + this keyword = strong position.

8. **"motorcycle maintenance tracker"** — Some apps support motorcycles but don't keyword-target it. If WrenchLog supports any vehicle type, this is free traffic.

9. **"fleet maintenance log"** — AUTOsist targets fleets but "fleet maintenance log" specifically is undertargeted.

10. **"EV maintenance tracker"** — Electric vehicles have different maintenance needs. Only Simply Auto and Carly partially address this.

## Recommended ASO Strategy for WrenchLog

### App Title (30 chars max)
```
WrenchLog: Car Maintenance Log
```
**Rationale:** Brand name first for memorability. "Car Maintenance Log" combines the #1 keyword ("car maintenance") with a gap keyword ("log"). Fits within 30 characters.

### App Subtitle (30 chars max)
```
Service Tracker & Oil Reminders
```
**Rationale:** Stacks "Service Tracker" (maps to "vehicle maintenance tracker", "car service") and "Oil Reminders" (maps to "oil change reminder"). Natural reading that doesn't feel keyword-stuffed.

**Alternative subtitles to A/B test:**
- `Vehicle Service Log & Tracker` (27 chars)
- `Mileage, Fuel & Service Log` (28 chars)
- `Car Service & Expense Tracker` (30 chars)

### Keyword Field (100 chars, comma-separated, iOS only)

```
car,maintenance,vehicle,tracker,fuel,mileage,expense,repair,service,reminder,oil,change,auto,history
```

**Character count:** 100 (exactly at limit)

**Coverage map:**
- `car maintenance` → title + keywords
- `vehicle maintenance tracker` → keywords + subtitle
- `oil change reminder` → subtitle + keywords
- `car service log` → title + subtitle + keywords
- `fuel tracker` → keywords
- `mileage tracker` → keywords
- `car expense` → keywords
- `car repair` → keywords
- `auto maintenance` → keywords
- `service history` → keywords + subtitle
- `vehicle tracker` → keywords

**Note:** Apple combines words from title, subtitle, and keyword field to form compound keywords. Individual words in the keyword field combine with title/subtitle words to create additional matchable phrases.

### Description Keyword Targets

Work these naturally into the first 3 lines of the App Store description (the visible portion before "more"):

- car maintenance tracker
- service log
- oil change reminder
- vehicle maintenance
- repair history
- fuel log
- mileage tracker
- maintenance schedule
- car expense tracker

### Category Selection

**Primary:** Utilities
**Secondary:** Lifestyle

**Rationale:** Utilities has less competition than Lifestyle for maintenance apps. Some competitors list under Lifestyle but the user intent is utility-first.

## Positioning & Differentiators for ASO Copy

Based on competitor weaknesses and user review sentiment:

| Differentiator | Why It Matters | Proof Points from Research |
|---|---|---|
| **Privacy-first** | CARFAX collects/sells data; users explicitly praise ServiceLog for not doing this | ServiceLog review: "it does not capture your data" |
| **No subscription required** | Carly gets 3.5★ on Google Play partly due to aggressive subscriptions | Multiple review complaints about subscription fatigue |
| **Modern SwiftUI interface** | VMT and older apps look dated; ServiceLog praised for "modern UI" | Users call out "sleek and modern" as positive |
| **Works offline** | Several competitors require accounts or cloud | aCar praised for offline capability |
| **DIY mechanic friendly** | "WrenchLog" name naturally signals hands-on car care | Gap: no competitor explicitly targets DIY audience |
| **Lifetime purchase option** | Users "love the one-time fee" model | ServiceLog review: "I'd rather pay a higher one-time fee than a subscription" |

## Localization Priorities

For initial launch, focus on English-language markets:

1. **US** (primary) — Largest iOS market; "car maintenance" terminology
2. **UK** — "car service" preferred over "car maintenance"; "MOT reminder" is a unique UK keyword
3. **Canada** — Bilingual (EN/FR); similar to US
4. **Australia** — "rego reminder" is unique; "car service log"

Future: German, Spanish, French (Drivvo supports 60+ languages; this is their moat)
